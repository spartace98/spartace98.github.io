    • fingerprintApril Tag Detection for Soft Robotics Hand Manipulations
      I have been working with Professor Nancy Pollard person from the Robotics Institute to design a soft robotics hand for dexterous manipulations.

      In order to test the robotic's hand ability to grab and perform manipulations to the object, the hand must be able to detect the object.

      April Tags are unique and easily detectable with any commerical camera. Current libraries are capable of providing dimensions and position of the detected tag, with careful camera calibration.



      To test feedback between the camera and the computer, I simulated a complex racing game which can be controlled by the movements of the April Tag.



    • cameraBlob Detection for General Applications
      It is useful to detect any given object for general applications.

      For objects with isolated color, without machine learning techniques to understand the texture, it is difficult to analyse the angle of the given object.

      A meaningful first step that has application, however, is to detect its position from a given image with color calibration to isolate the given object from the surroundings.



      I further tested the feedback between the real time object detection and performance of a given task.



      My project has demonstrate that while it is possible to perform a task with object detection of a general object, further research can be done to improve the efficiency of this feedback.

    • local_hospital Semisupervised learning on unilabel, multiclass, class imbalance environment
      Semi-supervised learning is an approach to machine learning that combines a small amount of labeled data with a large amount of unlabeled data during training, thus increasing the size of the training set. A large rtraining set improves the performance of deep neural networks.

      Former state of the art semi-supervised model, Mean Teacher, trains with a student and a teacher network, where the loss is backpropagated to the stduent network and the teacher weights are updated by the student's weight with an exponential moving average. However, this leads to confirmation bias, which means that the wrong learning by the student model gets propagated to the teacher model.

      Semi-supervised learning is also known to have poor performance in a class imbalanced environment (Hyun, M., Jeong, J., & Kwak, N. (2020). Class-Imbalanced Semi-Supervised Learning.)

      In medical datasets, data tend not to distributed among patients since most patients are healthy. A simple breakdown of the NIH Chest X-Ray dataset clearly illustrates this
      Chest X-ray images from NIH-14 dataset

      Cardiomegaly Hernia Atelectasis No Finding
      2772 227 11535 60412


      Therefore, the aim of my research was to develop a semi-supervised learning technique that has the capacity to perform better than a supervised learning model with just a subset of the data, in such a class imbalance environment. For further details, please contact me due to intellectual property rights.

    • bubble_chart Antiferromganetic Skyrmions for racetrack memory devices application
      Skyrmions are magnetic nanostructures that are stabilised by Exchange Energy and DMI Interaction.

      Current driven dynamics of skyrmions has been intensely studied due to their application in racetrack memory devices.

      The Skyrmions team at Institute of Material Research and Engineering (IMRE) headed by Professor Anjan Soumyanarayanan person studies the possibility of using anti-ferromagnetic materials to designi skyrmion based racetrack devices.

      With a theorectical design for the antiferromagnetic layers, I was involved in the effort of determining if the proposed structure can host stable magnetic skyrmions, through both simulations and experiments.

      Simulated Images
      Microscopy Image
      By analysing the periodicity of the stripes form with proper image analysis techniques, I was able to match them with actual experimental obervations of the stripes at a fixed magnetic field. This would thus allow me to verify that the parameters to generate this simulation is close to the actual one.

      For further details, please contact me due to intellectual property rights.
    • tap_and_play NUS Covid-19 Response Project
      Over the summer of 2020, I was invited by Professor John Ho from NUS Electrical and Computer Engineering Department to help with a national project aimed at assisting medical volunteers working frontline for infected patients in the local foreign worker dormitories.

      The purpose of the project was to reduce the frequency of contact between medical volunteers and patients during health monitoring, thus keeping both parties safe. This is done by connecting Oximiter devices to the cloud so that the patients can submit their blood oxygen readings directly without any contact with others.

      I was involved in the hardware, software and media components. With Javascript, I helped integrate blinking LEDs to the WIFI connected Raspberry Pi Zero, which indicates to the patients when the readings were sucessfully uploaded.


      I also helped to create the website that served 2 purposes.

        1. Train the foreign workers on how to use the devices
        2. Educate the community about the efforts between government and academic institutions towards solving the crisis
